The Story Behind

Inspired by the ancient root systems of the Appalachian forest, this vessel is hand-thrown and individually carved. Each piece takes three weeks to complete, undergoing a double-firing process in a custom wood-kiln that creates its unique, unpredictable depth of color.

Meet Elena Vance

“The clay speaks a language of time. When I sit at the wheel, I am not just making a vessel; I am continuing a conversation that started in my grandmother’s kitchen in the Appalachian foothills.”

Village Story

Rooted in the high peaks of Asheville, Elena’s studio is a sanctuary for the traditional techniques of wood-firing, a process she revived in the late 90s to honor local heritage.

The Craft History

Every glaze used is formulated from local minerals and plant ash, ensuring that every piece of CraftRoots pottery is literally a part of the land it came from.
